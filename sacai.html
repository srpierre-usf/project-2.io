SACAI

SPRING '24

This collection, under the creative direction of Chitose Abe, navigated the boundary between tailoring and workwear, reframing conventional garments through a lens of architectural hybridization. The runway quietly showcased layered compositions: pinstripe suits emerging under chore coat silhouettes, rugged denim and utility jackets reworked with finesse. Among the most striking pieces were the double-breasted chore style coat (a subtle nod to the Carhartt WIP collaboration), chunky leather loafers/derbies that reinterpret street footwear, and knitwear layered with unexpected textures that elevate simple sweaters into structural statements. The collection did not present a singular narrative but rather embodied the brand’s signature of “molecular” design assembly and reassembly to produce garments that feel both familiar and entirely new.
